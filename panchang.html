Understanding Panchang

What is Panchang?

Panchang is a Hindu calendar and almanac that provides important astrological information for each day. The word "Panchang" is derived from two Sanskrit words: "Pancha" meaning five and "Anga" meaning parts. These five elements are:

  • Tithi - Lunar day based on the position of the Moon relative to the Sun
  • Nakshatra - Constellation or lunar mansion where the Moon is located
  • Yoga - Auspicious or inauspicious period based on the combined longitude of the Sun and Moon
  • Karana - Half of a Tithi, used to determine the quality of the day
  • Vaar - Day of the week

Importance of Panchang

Panchang is used to determine the most auspicious times for various activities such as weddings, housewarming ceremonies, starting new ventures, and religious rituals. It helps in avoiding inauspicious periods and maximizing positive outcomes.

Key Auspicious Periods

  • Abhijit Muhurat: Considered the most auspicious time of the day
  • Amrit Kaal: Nectar period, highly favorable for important tasks
  • Brahma Muhurat: Ideal for meditation and spiritual practices

Key Inauspicious Periods

  • Rahu Kaal: Inauspicious period ruled by Rahu
  • Yamaganda: Period ruled by Yama, the god of death
  • Gulika Kaal: Inauspicious period associated with Saturn
