Essential palmistry knowledge to understand what your hands reveal about you.

Dominant Hand

Your dominant hand shows your present and future, while the non-dominant hand reveals innate tendencies and past influences.

Changing Lines

Palm lines can change over time, reflecting personal growth, life changes, and decisions that alter your life path.

Mounts

The fleshy pads at the base of your fingers (mounts) reveal planetary influences and specific personality traits.

Fingerprints

Fingerprint patterns (whorls, loops, arches) provide additional insights into your personality and behavioral patterns.

Hand Shape

The overall shape of your hand (earth, air, fire, water) indicates your elemental nature and basic approach to life.

Special Marks

Stars, crosses, squares, and islands on palm lines have specific meanings and can indicate special events or challenges.

Common questions about palmistry and our consultation process.

How accurate is palmistry?

Palmistry can provide remarkably accurate insights when performed by experienced practitioners. While it doesn't predict fixed events, it reveals tendencies, potentials, and likely outcomes based on current paths. The lines can change as you make different life choices.

Which hand should be read - left or right?

For right-handed people, the left hand shows innate potential and characteristics you're born with, while the right hand shows your current life and future developments. For left-handed people, this is reversed. We always analyze both hands for a complete reading.

Can palm lines change over time?

Yes, palm lines can and do change throughout your life. Minor lines may appear or disappear, and even major lines can develop branches or change their course. This reflects personal growth, changing circumstances, and the impact of your decisions on your life path.

How do I take good palm photos for an online reading?

For the best results: 1) Use natural daylight if possible, 2) Ensure your entire palm is in focus, 3) Take photos of both palms with fingers slightly spread, 4) Include close-ups of any specific areas you have questions about, 5) Make sure the lines are clearly visible without shadows.
